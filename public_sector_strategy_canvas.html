1 Public Value Proposition
What specific outcome do you produce that would not occur without your intervention? For whom?
State the outcome, not the activity. "Reduced disease burden in elderly population through early detection" not "we run screening programmes."
1a Why You, Not the Market?
What market failure or institutional capability justifies government intervention here?
Name the specific failure: information asymmetry? Public good? Externality? Equity gap? If you can't name it, the private sector may serve this need better.
1b Authorising Environment
Who defines what "public value" means for your mandate? What is the source of your legitimacy?
Minister? Parliament? Inter-agency committee? Public sentiment? Be specific about who you're accountable to and what they've authorised.
2 Tailored Activities — What You Do and How
List your mandate-specific activities. Each must require institutional capabilities unavailable to private/nonprofit actors. If it doesn't, it's a candidate for outsourcing.
For each activity: What is it? → Why must government do this? → What unique asset do you bring (data, regulatory authority, convening power, coercive authority)?
2a Standardised / Outsourced
What do you use government-wide standards or external providers for?
List activities where tailoring adds no value: procurement, IT infra, HR, finance. If mandate-specific activities appear here, you may be under-investing.
3 Trade-Offs — What You Choose NOT to Do
This is the hardest box and the most important. If you cannot fill the right column, you do not have a strategy. You have a to-do list.
WE DO — and invest deeply in
List 3–5 choices where you concentrate resources. These should be specific enough that someone could disagree with them.
WE DO NOT — and accept the consequences
List 3–5 things you deliberately refuse to do, even though they are defensible and some stakeholders want them. Name the stakeholder who will be unhappy.
4 Fit — How Activities Reinforce Each Other
Draw the connections. Does performing Activity A well make Activity B more effective? If activities sit independently, you have a portfolio of programmes, not an integrated strategy.

Describe how each connection works. "Intelligence from Activity 1 directly determines which cohorts we fund in Activity 3." Not "these are all related."
✓ How You Know It's Working
Outcome metrics with counterfactuals, not activity metrics. "Number trained" is activity. "Incremental wage uplift vs control group at 18 months" is outcome.
For each tailored activity: what outcome do you measure? What's the counterfactual? Over what time horizon? What would make you shut this down?
5 Continuity — What Stays Stable
What is the enduring core position vs. what adapts? Strategy needs years to compound. Tactics change quarterly.
Core position (stable 5+ years): e.g. "We focus on market-excluded segments, not general workforce."
Adaptive layer (evolves): e.g. "Which sectors, which skills, which programme modalities."
Uniqueness Test

If you removed your agency name, could this canvas describe any other agency? If yes, it's not a strategy.

Trade-off Test

Is the "We Do Not" column filled with specific, uncomfortable choices? If it's empty or vague, you haven't made trade-offs.

Counterfactual Test

Would your key outcomes occur anyway without your intervention? If yes, you're consuming resources for zero incremental value.

Fit Test

If you removed one activity, would others degrade? If activities are independent, you have a portfolio, not a system.

Full Cost Test

Are you counting compliance costs imposed on others, not just your budget? If not, your ROIC is fiction.
